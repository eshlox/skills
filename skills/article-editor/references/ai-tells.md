# AI Tells: Checklists

Search the draft for these. Each hit is a candidate for deletion or a plain-word swap — not an automatic crime. Skilled humans use some of these too; the density is the signal, not any single occurrence. Never use these lists to accuse anyone of using AI; use them only to improve prose.

## Tell-words (peer-reviewed lists: Kobak et al., Science Advances 2025; Juzek & Ward, COLING 2025)

delve / delving, tapestry, realm, landscape, testament, underscore, showcase / showcasing, pivotal, crucial, vital, paramount, compelling, meticulous, commendable, intricate, nuanced, robust, seamless, holistic, comprehensive, multifaceted, vibrant, unwavering, resonate, foster, harness, unlock, empower, elevate, embark, navigate, leverage, boast, surpass, notably, moreover, furthermore, alignment

Typical swaps: delve into → look at; leverage → use; utilize → use; crucial/pivotal → important (or delete); foster → build/encourage; navigate → handle/deal with; robust → solid; seamless → smooth (or delete); showcase → show; comprehensive → full (or delete); in the realm of → in.

## Filler phrases (Pangram Labs frequency data; Wikipedia:Signs of AI writing)

- "it's important to note that" / "it's worth noting that" → delete; just say the thing
- "in today's fast-paced world" / "in the ever-evolving landscape of" → delete
- "when it comes to X" → "for X" or restructure
- "a testament to" → delete or state the fact directly
- "plays a crucial/pivotal/vital role in" → "matters for" / "helps"
- "navigate the complexities of" → "handle" / "deal with"
- "at the end of the day" → delete
- "unlock the potential of" → delete or say what actually happens
- "serves as" → "is"
- "generally speaking" and other hedging preambles → delete unless the hedge carries real meaning

## Formulaic constructions

- **Negation pivot**: "It's not just X, it's Y" / "It's not about X, it's about Y". Rewrite as a direct statement of Y.
- **False range**: "From X to Y, ..." pretending two examples span everything. Just give the examples.
- **Reflexive rule-of-three**: every point delivered as a triplet ("clear, concise, and compelling"). Keep threes that earn it; break the pattern elsewhere.
- **Staccato triplet**: "No fluff. No filler. No nonsense." One is fine per article; more is a tic.
- **Throat-clearing openers**: "In today's world...", "Great question!", restating the topic before saying anything. Cut to the first real sentence.
- **Formulaic closers**: "In conclusion", "In summary", vague optimism ("The future looks bright..."). End on a specific point instead.

## Structural tells

- Uniform sentence length ("low burstiness"). Fix: mix sub-10-word sentences with 20–40-word ones; never 3+ similar-length sentences in a row. This is the single highest-leverage humanizing fix.
- Uniform paragraph length. Vary it.
- Bolded list-headers followed by colons on every bullet; over-bolding generally.
- Bullet lists where prose would flow better (and Title Case on every heading).
- Mechanical transitions opening every paragraph (Furthermore / Moreover / Additionally).
- Generalization where a human would particularize. Fix only with specifics the author already provided.
- Em-dash clusters — a *weak* signal; thin out 3–4 per paragraph, don't eliminate.

## What NOT to do

Do not "humanize" by inserting typos, forced fragments, or slang the author wouldn't use — over-correction is itself a detectable pattern and it damages the text. The goal is the author's natural voice, cleaner.
